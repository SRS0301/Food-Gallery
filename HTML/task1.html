KERALA

Know Your Motherland

Kerala is a state on the southwestern coast of India, known for its palm-lined beaches, backwaters, and lush green landscapes. Its diverse geography includes the Western Ghats, which are rich in biodiversity and home to several wildlife sanctuaries. The state experiences a tropical climate, with heavy monsoons during the rainy season. Kerala’s backwaters, a network of interconnected canals, rivers, and lakes, are a major tourist attraction, offering houseboat cruises and scenic views. The state's capital is Thiruvananthapuram, and Kochi is a major commercial hub.
Kerala is also known for its high literacy rate, progressive social policies, and a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam. It has a long history of trade and cultural exchange, with influences from Arab, Chinese, and European traders. The state is also famous for its festivals like Onam and Vishu, and its cuisine, which features dishes like appam, sambar, and seafood. Kerala’s economy is driven by agriculture, tourism, and remittances from its large expatriate community in the Middle East.

MYTH about its Origin

According to Hindu mythology, Kerala was created by Parasurama, the sixth avatar of Lord Vishnu. It is said that Parasurama threw his axe into the Arabian Sea from the top of the Western Ghats, and the sea receded to reveal a stretch of fertile land, which became Kerala. This legend gives Kerala the nickname "God's Own Country." Parasurama is also believed to have reclaimed the land to provide a place for Brahmins to live and practice their rituals, shaping Kerala's early religious and cultural landscape.
